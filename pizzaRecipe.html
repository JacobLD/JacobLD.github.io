    Dough - makes 2 pizzas worth

  • 3 3/4 Cups Flour
  • 1 1/2 Cups Warm Water
  • 1/2 tbs + 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp ActiveDry Yeast

    • Sauce

  • 5 Cloves of Garlic - Pressed/Minced
  • 28oz Crushed or Diced Tomatoes
  • 1/2 tbs + 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Crushed Red Pepper - to taste

    • Cheese

  • 8oz block of Mozzarella per pizza- Grated

Prep - Sauce 1 hour

  1. Combine all ingredients in a sauce pan. Note not to add too much red pepper. A little goes a long way.
  2. Bring to a boil and let simmer for 1 hour.
  • While that simmers lets get started on the dough.

Prep - Dough 1 hour 15 mins

  1. Combine all dough ingredients - only until all ingredients are mixed via hand or mixer.
  2. Roll edges until dough is a dome shape.
  3. Place dough in container that will allow it to double in size - you may want to oil the container first. Cover in plastic wrap and let rest until doubled in size.
  4. Roll dough again into a dome shape. Let rest for 45 mins
  5. It's best to leave the dough in the fridge overnight before baking but it is ready to bake now - note it will be harder to work with while warm. Another option is to prep the dough early in the morning and let sit in the fridge until you're ready to make the pizza. If you are going the fridge route make sure to split the dough into two balls before placing in the fridge.

Directions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 400° F
  2. For the final construction of the pizza you will need: Sauce, Dough, Cheese, Oil, butter and a cookie sheet to bake the pizza in.
  3. Place a small amount of oil in the cookie sheet and spread around with your hands.
  4. Once the cookie sheet is covered take half the dough you have prepared and place it into the cookie sheet and push it down into the sheet until it fills the whole sheet edge to edge. This is by far the hardest part and is made much easier by cooling the dough first.
  5. We are all done with the oil so you can wash up. Spread the sauce around on the dough. How much is up to you but I prefer more sauce as opposed to less.
  6. Take the grated cheese and sprinkle over the sauce.
  7. Now is a good time to add any extra toppings you may like.
  8. Last touch is to take some melted butter and brush it over the exposed dough. It is fine not to do this but it can make the crust worth while.
  9. Place that soggy pie in the oven for about 10 mins and then check. Everyones oven is different so checking at 3-5 min intervals after 10 mins will help narrow down a good cook time. Remove once crust is golden on the bottom.
  10. Remove from oven and cut to serve.
  11. If you are serving for more than 3 people go ahead and use the rest of that dough to make a second one while you munch on the first.

Notes

  • A yummy variation of this pizza is to replace the sauce with bbq sauce and add ground beef.
  • The sauce benefits greatly from fresh tomatoes. If you can manage to crush your own it will cut the acidity down a ton.
  • If the sauce boils all the water out it is fine to add small amounts of water back.
